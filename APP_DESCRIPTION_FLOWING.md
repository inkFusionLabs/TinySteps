## TinySteps: NICU Dad's Companion

TinySteps is a comprehensive mobile application designed specifically for fathers whose babies are in the Neonatal Intensive Care Unit. This essential companion app provides emotional support, practical tools, and comprehensive resources to help dads navigate one of life's most challenging experiences with confidence and support.

The app addresses the unique challenges faced by NICU dads, including emotional overwhelm, lack of specialized support, difficulty tracking baby's progress, and the isolation and stress that comes with long hospital stays. TinySteps serves as both a practical tool and an emotional support system, offering mental health resources and encouragement throughout the NICU journey.

At its core, TinySteps features four main sections that work together to provide comprehensive support. The Home Dashboard offers a quick overview of baby's daily progress with encouragement messages tailored specifically for NICU dads, along with immediate access to UK support resources through direct calling functionality. The Progress Tracking section provides visual charts showing baby's growth and development, milestone celebrations for important achievements, and growth predictions based on current data to help dads see their baby's journey forward.

The Daily Journal section allows for private journaling where dads can process their thoughts and feelings, attach photos to capture precious moments, and access mental health support integrated throughout the experience. The NICU Information Hub serves as a comprehensive medical glossary with dad-friendly explanations, UK support resources and contact information, emergency contacts with direct calling, and healthcare provider information and appointments.

What sets TinySteps apart is its comprehensive UK support integration, featuring over twenty UK support organizations including Bliss Charity, NHS 111, Samaritans, Mind, DadPad, and NHS Healthier Together. The app provides one-tap calling to support organizations, crisis support with immediate access, mental health resources available twenty-four seven, and local support groups and information specifically tailored for UK users.
